I went to Home Depot the other morning to get a grinding disc in anticipation of a project. Well I bought a grinding disc I already had, but I did not buy the one I needed!

Since my last move, my grinding discs have been tucked away in several boxes in a cabinet. This made it really hard to do a general assessment or find a disc. That needed to change.

As it turned out, I had the perfect pieces cut to size in the scrap bin. I did do a quick <a href="https://cad.onshape.com/documents/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/w/1890f058e50a78405dbb7900/e/8f715ca3e3cd102d16f5364b" target="_blank">cad layout in Onshape</a>.

I put a slight angle on the rods using my home bender. I just made the first one and used it as a gauge for the next ones.

<a href="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/4f95e4cb1483ff54cbddda66?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.5552732376140976" target="_blank"><img src="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/4f95e4cb1483ff54cbddda66?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.5552732376140976" alt="build start" width="630"/></a>

I tacked it all up, but it did not work.

<a href="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/4452997da18a4669890500da?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.8214182172574567" target="_blank"><img src="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/4452997da18a4669890500da?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.8214182172574567" alt="build start" width="630"/></a>

Forgot to account for the slop in the hole.

<a href="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/970b724384e264655c697fd3?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.9774712556188832" target="_blank"><img src="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/970b724384e264655c697fd3?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.9774712556188832" alt="build start" width="630"/></a>

So I cut them off and redid them and called it good.

<a href="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/11cff257f9c4d67cc7cef7bf?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.9071133890622491" target="_blank"><img src="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/11cff257f9c4d67cc7cef7bf?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.9071133890622491" alt="build start" width="630"/></a>

Then I realized I needed something to hold the wrenches. First thought of welding on a small rod, but you would have to take all the wrenches off to get to the inner wrench. No bueno.

<a href="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/9b42092096f8cf31858c2c53?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.9267331162520651" target="_blank"><img src="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/9b42092096f8cf31858c2c53?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.9267331162520651" alt="build start" width="630"/></a>

So more tubing!

<a href="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/8ec07d9cdb2848e539e77263?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.19029298694242813" target="_blank"><img src="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/8ec07d9cdb2848e539e77263?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.19029298694242813" alt="build start" width="630"/></a>

I threw some old paint on it.

<a href="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/d4cdbd31d675dc29829690ed?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.2708115188020266" target="_blank"><img src="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/d4cdbd31d675dc29829690ed?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.2708115188020266" alt="build start" width="630"/></a>

<a href="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/5dbc9058f8198bc740d3ecfb?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.6228066012312852" target="_blank"><img src="https://cad.onshape.com/api/elements/download/051d3a49fa876088d094ccb1/5dbc9058f8198bc740d3ecfb?workspaceId=1890f058e50a78405dbb7900&random=0.6228066012312852" alt="build start" width="630"/></a>

And it is done! And I know exactly what grinding discs I have!
